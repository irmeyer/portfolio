IUB Nature Immersion Experience - Usability Research & Design

The IUB Nature Immersion Experience is a usability research project aimed at enhancing engagement with Indiana University's existing plant life database. This project focused on refining an interactive map and mobile application, allowing users to explore and learn about campus greenery in a more intuitive and immersive way.

Project Goals:

  • Improve user interaction with IU's plant database.
  • Enhance navigation and accessibility of tree and plant information through digital tools.
  • Encourage environmental awareness and engagement with campus nature.

Research & Evaluation Methods:

  • Think-Aloud Protocols - Captured real-time user feedback while navigating the application.
  • Timed Task Analysis - Measured efficiency in performing key interactions (e.g., identifying plant species, locating information).
  • Open-Ended User Feedback - Gathered qualitative insights to refine usability and functionality.

Key Findings & Design Solutions:

  • Users struggled with map navigation and information clarity, leading to improved layout and labeling.
  • The mobile interface lacked intuitive guidance, prompting the addition of onboarding instructions and clearer visual cues.
  • The QR code system was underutilized, leading to enhancements in its explanation and integration with the interactive experience.
  • Map readability and accessibility improvements were made to ensure better engagement across different user groups.
