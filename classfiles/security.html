INFO - I430 : Security of Networked Systems

Public Key Infrastructure (PKI) Failures (INFO-I430 Final Paper)

This research paper explores vulnerabilities and failures in Public Key Infrastructure (PKI), focusing on the challenges in digital certificate management and cybersecurity threats.

Key Insights:

  • PKI Overview: Explains Certificate Authorities (CAs) and their role in trust and authentication on the internet.
  • Research Methodology: Analyzes Bugzilla security reports using qualitative coding to identify failure causes in PKI implementations.

Common PKI Failures:

  • Human Error: The leading cause of misconfigurations.
  • Compliance Issues: Non-adherence to Baseline Requirements (BRs).
  • Weak Cryptographic Practices: Usage of outdated hashing algorithms and rogue certificates.
  • Delayed Certificate Revocation & Security Breaches.

Solutions & Recommendations:

  • Better auditing and monitoring of PKI infrastructure.
  • Enhanced education and training for IT security teams.
  • Root cause analysis and redundancy strategies for disaster recovery.
  • This paper highlights critical security challenges in PKI, emphasizing the need for better compliance, automation, and awareness in cybersecurity.
