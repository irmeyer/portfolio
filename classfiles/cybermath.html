INFO - I231 : Mathematics of Cybersecurity

Homework 5 (INFO-I231 Cryptography & Security)

This document contains five questions related to cryptographic techniques, including Data Encryption Standard (DES), Group Theory, and the Hill Cipher.

Topics Covered:

  • DES Permutation: Applies expansion mapping and permutation techniques.
  • Hill Cipher Decryption: Computes matrix inverse, determinant, and applies decryption to recover plaintext.
  • Matrix-Based Encryption: Attempts to decrypt another Hill Cipher-based encryption but finds an invalid determinant, making decryption impossible.
  • Group Theory in Cryptography: Defines modular arithmetic properties like closure, associativity, commutativity, and inverses.
  • S-Box Substitutions: Performs bitwise transformations using substitution boxes (S-boxes). This homework demonstrates proficiency in mathematical cryptography, particularly modular arithmetic, matrix-based encryption, and classic cipher techniques.
